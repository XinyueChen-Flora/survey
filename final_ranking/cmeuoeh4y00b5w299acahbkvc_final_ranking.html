Plant-Based Meat Alternatives – Competitive Landscape Report

1. Market Context

The U.S. plant-based meat market was valued at USD 3.21B in 2024 and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 18.1% through 2030 (Grand View Research). While topline growth has decelerated from the early boom years (NIQ notes a “slowdown” in 2024), the category retains strong fundamentals:

  • Health- and sustainability-driven demand: Consumers are seeking lower saturated fat/cholesterol options with environmental benefits.
  • Flexitarian adoption: Growth is now driven more by omnivores/flexitarians than strict vegans/vegetarians.
  • Price premium resilience: Despite inflationary pressures, plant-based products have maintained pricing above conventional meat.
  • High product visibility: Search volume and merchandising visibility for plant-based SKUs continue to rise, making discovery easier.

The competitive environment is marked by both branded specialist players and diversified CPG portfolios extending into the segment. Barriers to entry remain moderate, with innovation cycles centered on improving taste, texture, nutrition, and clean-label credentials.

  • Beyond Meat leverages storytelling around health and environmental benefits, while pushing into adjacent protein types. Its challenge is balancing premium positioning with broader household penetration as more affordable options emerge.
